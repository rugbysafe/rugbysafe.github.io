Graduated Return To Play Protocol - GRTP

Injury Date

Confirm the date of your injury on the left

Expectation

You have been removed from play under the recognised and remove program. This calculator will provide you with the earliest date you can return to play. At stage 2A you should have no further concussion symptoms before proceeding to the next stages.

Stage 1

Initial Rest (Body & Brain)

Objective

Recovery

Activity Allowed

No driving or exercise. Minimise screen time. Consider time off or adaption of study/work.

Duration

No training

% Max Heart Rate

No training

Stage 2a

Relative Rest (Symptom lmited activities)

Objective

Return to normal activities ( as symptoms permit).
No symptoms at the end of the 14 days

Activity Allowed

Initially daily activites that do not provoke symptoms. Consider time off or adaption of study/work

Duration

No training

% Max Heart Rate

No training

Stage 2b

Light aerobic exercise

Objective

Increase heart rate

Activity Allowed

Light jogging for 10-15 minutes, swimming or stationary cycling at low to moderate intensity. No resistence training

Duration

Less than 20 mins

% Max Heart Rate

Less than 70%

Stage 3

Sport-Specific Exercise

Objective

Add movement

Activity Allowed

For example:Running drills. No head impact activities

Duration

Less than 45 mins

% Max Heart Rate

Less than 80%

Stage 4

Non-contact Training

Objective

Exercise, coordination anf cognitive load. A return to learning/work must be achieved before returning to sport

Activity Allowed

For example:Passing drills. May start progressive resistance training

Duration

Less than 60 mins

% Max Heart Rate

Less than 90%

Stage 5

Full Contact Practice

Objective

Restore confidence and assess functional skills

Activity Allowed

Following medical review, return to normal training activities

Stage 6

Return to Play

Objective

Exercise, coordination and cognitive load

Activity Allowed

Normal game play
